## Overview

YouTube is a large-scale video streaming platform owned by Google, used by over 2 billion people to upload, watch, and share videos across the world.

<Callout kind="info">

This quickstart walks you through three core actions on YouTube: watching a video, uploading a video, and sharing a video.

</Callout>

## 1. Watch a video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open YouTube" icon="monitor">

    Go to the YouTube homepage in your browser or open the YouTube app on your device.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Find something to watch" icon="search">

    Use the search bar to type a topic, creator name, or video title, or browse the homepage recommendations.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Start playback" icon="play-circle">

    Click or tap a video thumbnail to open the watch page. The video will start playing automatically; use the controls to pause, adjust volume, or change quality.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## 2. Upload a video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your video" icon="settings">

    Make sure your video is saved on your device in a common format and that you have the rights to upload and share it.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Begin the upload" icon="upload">

    From YouTube, select the create button, then choose the option to upload a video. Pick the video file from your device.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Add details" icon="edit">

    Enter a clear title and description, choose a thumbnail, and select the audience and visibility options that match how you want others to see your video.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish" icon="check-circle">

    Confirm your settings and publish the video so it becomes available on YouTube.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## 3. Share a video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the video" icon="monitor">

    Go to the watch page of the video you want to share.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Use the share options" icon="share">

    Select the share button below the video to copy a link or share directly to supported apps and platforms.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Send to others" icon="users">

    Paste the link in a message, email, or post so friends, family, or your audience can watch the video on YouTube.

  </Step>
</Steps>
