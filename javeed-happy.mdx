## Overview

YouTube is a large-scale video streaming platform owned by Google, used by more than 2 billion people worldwide. It allows anyone to upload videos, watch and share content, and stream video globally across a wide range of devices.

<Callout kind="info">

  This page gives you a friendly, high-level overview of what YouTube is for and how people typically use it. It does not cover internal implementation details or APIs.

</Callout>

## Core capabilities

YouTube focuses on a few simple, powerful actions:

- **Upload videos** so creators can share content with a global audience.
- **Watch videos** on demand, from short clips to long-form content.
- **Share content** through links, embeds, and playlists.
- **Stream globally** so viewers can access videos from almost anywhere with an internet connection.

## Who YouTube is for

YouTube serves several types of users:

- **Viewers** who want to discover, watch, and share videos.
- **Creators** who upload videos and build an audience.
- **Communities** that gather around shared interests, channels, or topics.
- **Organizations and brands** that use video to reach and engage audiences.

## Typical user journey

<Steps>
  <Step title="Discover a video" icon="search">

    A user searches for a topic or browses recommendations and finds a video that matches their interests.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch and interact" icon="play-circle">

    The user watches the video and may like it, comment, or subscribe to the channel to see more similar content.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload and share" icon="upload">

    As a creator, the user records a video, uploads it to YouTube with a title and description, and shares the link or embeds it on other sites.

  </Step>
</Steps>
